Edikaikong Soup

Edikangikong Soup is a vegetable soup that originated among the Efik people of Cross River State and Akwa Ibom State in Southeastern Nigeria. It is considered to be a delicacy among some Nigerians, and is sometimes served during occasions of importance.

Serving (12 persons)

Ingredients                  Measurements
Pumpkin leaves  1kg
Water Leaf  200g
Assorted Meat  500g
Smoked Fish  200g
Palm Oil  250ml
Periwinkle  1cup
Ground Creayfish  2 tablespoons
Stock Cubes  2
Onion  1 Medium Size
Scotch Bonnet Pepper  2
Salt  To taste

Steps to Prepare Edikaikong

  1. Prepare the vegetables by washing and cutting them into tiny bits(thin slices preferably) and set aside
  2. Wash and cut the assorted meat in bite-able sizes. Clean the periwinkles, dried fish and stock fish and set aside
  3. Place the assorted meat and stock fish in the pot , add a little water,the chopped onion and one stock cube
  4. When the meat is done, add the dried fish and periwinkle.Add the palm oil, the ground pepper and crayfish and leave to boil for about 10 minutes
  5. Add the water leaves and leave to cook for 3 minutes, then add the Ugu leaves(fluted pumpkin leaves) and salt to taste
  6. Stir thoroughly and leave to simmer for 5 minutes on low heat and your Edikangikong soup is ready. Enjoy!
